He was born in Hong Kong and raised in Vancouver, Canada. Charles began his career in the entertainment industry in the mid-1990s as a singer, releasing albums both in Cantonese and Mandarin. He later ventured into acting and appeared in various Hong Kong TV dramas, films, and stage productions. He is known for his roles in "Triumph in the Skies," "Lan Kwai Fong 3," and "The Menu." Charles is also a philanthropist and actively contributes to various charity organizations in Canada and Hong Kong.

Bryan Lee O'Malley

Bryan Lee O'Malley (February 21, 1979 London-) also known as Bryan Lee O'Malley, Kupek or Bryan O'Malley is a Canadian cartoonist, writer and actor.

He is best known for creating the Scott Pilgrim series of graphic novels, which were later adapted into a successful film in 2010. O'Malley began his career in the early 2000s with his first graphic novel, Lost at Sea, which gained critical acclaim. He has won multiple awards throughout his career, including the Joe Shuster Award for Outstanding Cartoonist in 2005 and the Doug Wright Award for Best Emerging Talent in 2004.
