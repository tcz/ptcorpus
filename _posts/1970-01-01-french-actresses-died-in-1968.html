Notably, she wrote, directed and produced over a thousand films most of which are considered lost. Some of these films include, The Cabbage Fairy (1896), A Fool and His Money (1912) and The Ocean Waif (1916). She also introduced the use of synchronized sound and color tinting in her films before they became widely used in the industry. In addition to her notable contributions in the film industry, she was also an advocate for gender equality in the workplace. Alice Guy-Blaché is remembered as a pioneer in the film industry who opened the doors for future generations of female directors and producers.
