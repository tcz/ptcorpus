Tiziano Terzani (September 14, 1938 Florence-July 28, 2004 Orsogna) was an Italian journalist.

Terzani was one of the most renowned Italian journalists, famous for his reporting of Asian affairs. He worked as a correspondent for the German newspaper Der Spiegel and was also a correspondent for the Italian newspaper Corriere della Sera. Terzani travelled widely throughout Asia, covering the Vietnam War, Communist China, and the fall of Saigon. He authored several books, including his popular works "A Fortune-Teller Told Me" and "Goodnight, Mr. Lenin".
