Astrid Lindgren's most famous literary work is the Pippi Longstocking series, featuring a mischievous and independent young girl named Pippi who lives alone with her horse and monkey. The first book in the series, "Pippi Longstocking," was published in 1945 and became an instant classic, translated into over 70 languages. Lindgren wrote over 30 other children's books, including the Emil of Lönneberga and Karlsson-on-the-Roof series. Lindgren was an advocate for children's rights and education and served as a board member of the Swedish Society for Nature Conservation. In honor of her contribution to children's literature, the Astrid Lindgren Memorial Award was established in 2002, becoming the world's largest children's literature award.
